Hollow Body Kick-Up Practice ▶ Video
Entry reliability. Hollow before feet leave floor — position is set before the kick, not after.
 2 × 8–10 attempts Slow setup each rep. Engage hollow, squeeze glutes, arms by ears — then kick. 20s between attempts. 60s between sets Recovery day so keep intensity low — this is pure pattern practice. Count how many of the 8–10 entries land in balance. Aim to improve that number each week. Don't chase the hold — just nail the entry.
KB Snatch or Clean & Press Practice ▶ Video
Skill maintenance. Power + coordination.
 10 min practice Unhurried. Focus on groove, not load. As needed This is practice, not training. Moderate weight. Stop if form degrades.
Ring Rows — Feet Elevated ▶ Video
Lat, upper back and rear delt mass. Different pulling angle to pull-ups. Home rings.
 2 × 8–12 reps 2s pull. 1s hold chest to rings. 3s lower. Body rigid throughout. 90s Feet on a chair or box — the more horizontal your body, the harder it is. Start with a moderate angle and progress by lowering your body angle over weeks. Rings turned out at the top. This is a recovery day so keep it at a moderate effort — not a grind.
Ring Push-Ups — Feet Elevated ▶ Video
Horizontal press with natural adduction at top. Upper chest emphasis with feet raised.
 2 × 8–12 reps 3s descent. 1s hold at bottom. 2s press. Hands naturally adduct and turn out at top. 60s Feet on chair shifts emphasis to upper chest. Allow hands to travel slightly together at the top — that's the adduction that makes ring push-ups superior to floor push-ups for pec development. Keep body rigid, don't let hips sag.
Ring Flyes ▶ Video
Stretched-position pec loading. The stimulus your plan is currently missing entirely.
 2 × 8–10 reps 3s lowering arms out wide. 1s hold at full stretch. 2s return bringing hands together. 90s Rings at chest height, body angled forward. Start conservatively — this movement is deceptively hard and the pec stretch under load can cause soreness if you overdo it first session. Slightly bent arms throughout — never fully locked. Progress by increasing body angle (more horizontal = harder).
Elevated Heel Pistol Squat Practice ▶ Video
Heel on a 2–3cm plate or wedge. Removes ankle restriction so you can learn correct position first.
 3 × 5 reps each side 4s descent. 2s hold at bottom. 3s return. Supported (post or band) if needed. 60s between sides Key positions to nail: chest leans toward knee as you descend (not upright). Free leg extends forward — hip flexors hold it up, don't let it drop. At bottom, weight through heel even if elevated. Gradually reduce heel height over weeks as these positions become natural.
Ankle Dorsiflexion Wall Drill ▶ Video
Calf + Achilles length under load. The specific range needed for flat-footed pistol.
 3 × 10 reps each side Lunge position, front foot 5–8cm from wall. Drive knee forward over toes to touch wall. Hold 2s. Return. 30s Gradually move foot further from wall as range improves. When you can drive knee to wall with foot 10–12cm away flat-footed, your ankle restriction is solved. Track your distance each session.
Hip Flexor Pistol Hold ▶ Video
Trains holding the free leg up and forward — the position people forget is half the battle.
 3 × 15–20s hold each side Stand on one leg, other leg extended straight forward at hip height. Hold. Torso slightly forward. 45s If you can't hold the free leg at hip height for 15s your hip flexors are the limiting factor, not just your ankle. This drill directly fixes the heel-lift compensation pattern.
Pistol work is 3x per week (Days 1, 3, 5) — not daily. Quality over frequency for this skill given the ankle and hip position work needed. Muscle-up micro-drill (5 min max) can be tagged onto any session daily — just the transition catch and one set of chest-to-bar. Keep it light enough that it doesn't affect your main work.

Exercise Progressions — Current Move → Next Move → When to Advance
Tuck Planche Hold
Days 1 · 4 · 6/7
Stage What changes Advance when...
★ Tuck Planche Hold
You are here
 Knees tucked tight to chest. Hips up. Upper back rounded. Straight arms, shoulders depressed and protracted. 3 × 12s hold with hips level to shoulders, arms fully straight, no wobbling or sinking. Feels controlled, not desperate.
Advanced Tuck
 Knees still tucked but back now flat and horizontal — not rounded. Hips higher. Much harder than it sounds. 3 × 10s with back truly parallel to floor and arms straight. Hips not dropping.
One-Leg Extension
 Advanced tuck but one leg extends straight back. Alternate legs. Bridge between tuck and straddle. 3 × 8s each side cleanly, hips level throughout.
Straddle Planche
 Both legs extended wide apart, body horizontal. This is where your pancake flexibility becomes essential. 3 × 8s. Then gradually narrow the straddle width over months toward full planche.
Ring False Grip Muscle-Up Progression
Day 2 lunch · daily micro-drill
Stage What changes Advance when...
★ False Grip Conditioning
You are here
 Wrist draped over ring in dead hang. Building wrist tolerance and grip security. Both hands. Can hold 3 × 30s in solid false grip without grip slipping or wrist pain. Grip feels secure, not precarious.
False Grip Ring Rows + Pull-Ups
Current training
 Pulling in false grip — rows first, then full pull-ups. Rings travel to lower chest. Elbows close. 4 × 5 false grip pull-ups with rings touching lower sternum at top. False grip maintained throughout every rep.
Assisted Ring Muscle-Up
 Band or jump assist through the transition. Full movement — pull, lean forward, press to support. Rings turned out at top. 5 smooth assisted reps with minimal band tension. Transition feels like a continuation of the pull, not a separate effort.
Negative Ring Muscle-Up
 Jump or press to support position, then lower slowly through the transition back to dead hang. 5s negative. 5 controlled negatives with full 5s descent. No crashing through the transition on the way down.
Strict Ring False Grip Muscle-Up
Goal
 Dead hang start, false grip, pull rings to chest, lean and press to locked support with rings turned out. No kip, no jump. The complete movement. First clean rep. Then build to 3, then 5. Then string multiple reps together.
Tuck Dragon Flag
Days 1 · 6/7
Stage What changes Advance when...
★ Tuck Dragon Flag
You are here
 Shoulders on bench, knees tucked to chest. Lower with 3s negative, hold, return. Hips up throughout. 3 × 6 reps with slow, controlled 3s negative and no hip drop. Or 3 × 10s static hold at horizontal.
Single-Leg Extension
 Tuck position but one leg extends straight. Alternate. Lengthens the lever progressively. 3 × 5 reps each side, controlled negative, no twisting.
Straddle Dragon Flag
 Both legs extended, straddled wide. Reduces lever compared to legs-together version. 3 × 4 reps with full straight-body negative. Hips in line with shoulders throughout.
Full Dragon Flag
 Both legs together, fully extended. Body rigid as a plank. Bruce Lee territory. 3 × 4 clean reps. Then add reps and eventually load (ankles weighted).
Floor Back Raises
Days 4 · 6/7
Stage What changes Advance when...
★ Floor Back Raises
You are here
 Prone on floor, arms by sides or extended. Raise chest and legs simultaneously. 3s up, 8s hold, 3s lower. 3 × 6 reps with 8s hold at top, arms fully extended overhead, feels easy and controlled.
Weighted Floor Back Raises
 Same movement but hold a light plate or KB behind the head. Increases spinal erector load directly. 3 × 6 with 5kg, 8s hold. Then increase load incrementally.
Back Extension (on bench/GHD)
 Hips over the edge of a bench or GHD machine. Full range of motion, greater stretch at the bottom. 3 × 8 bodyweight reps, full range. Then add load.
Weighted Back Extension
 Loaded back extension. This is the direct feeder for back lever strength and planche upper-spine engagement. Build load progressively. 3 × 8 at significant weight = very strong posterior chain.
Hanging Tuck Hold
Days 4 · 6/7
Stage What changes Advance when...
★ Hanging Tuck Hold
You are here
 Dead hang, knees pulled to chest. Active scapular depression — pull shoulder blades down, don't just hang passively. 3 × 12s hold, knees above hip height, fully active shoulders. No passive hanging at all.
Hanging L-Sit Hold
 Legs extend to horizontal — thighs parallel to floor, knees bent 90°. Greater hip flexor demand. 3 × 10s with thighs horizontal and active shoulders throughout.
Hanging Straight-Leg Raise to Hold
 Raise straight legs to horizontal and hold. Full hip flexor + lat compression. Feeds front lever directly. 3 × 8s hold with legs straight and horizontal. No swinging to get there.
Tuck Front Lever
 Body horizontal, arms straight, knees tucked. The beginning of front lever — a major milestone in its own right. 3 × 8s with body parallel to floor and straight arms. Then begin extending legs toward straddle front lever.
Pistol Squat Specialism
Days 3, 5 + Fingertip Lunge Days 5 · 6/7
Stage What changes Advance when...
★ Elevated Heel Pistol
You are here — fixing position first
 Heel on 2–3cm plate/wedge. Supported (band or post). Focus on: chest leans toward knee, free leg held high and forward, weight through heel at bottom. 5 controlled reps each side with correct body position — chest forward, free leg at hip height throughout. Heel not lifting even at full depth.
Reduce Heel Elevation
 Drop heel plate from ~3cm to ~1.5cm. Same movement, same positions. Ankle dorsiflexion now doing more work. 5 reps each side at reduced elevation, positions intact, heel flat on plate throughout.
Flat Foot Assisted Pistol
 No heel elevation. Band or post for balance only — not for pulling yourself up. Heel must stay flat through full range. 5 smooth reps each side, flat foot, minimal band tension used. You're doing 90%+ of the work.
Full Unassisted Pistol Squat
Goal
 No support, no elevation. Flat heel, chest forward, free leg held horizontal. The complete movement. 5 × 5 each side. Then add a goblet hold for loaded pistol work.
Hamstring Planks
Days 5 · 6/7
Stage What changes Advance when...
★ Two-Leg Hamstring Plank
You are here
 Heels on floor or low bench, hips raised, squeeze hamstrings and glutes to hold body straight. 10s holds. 3 × 15s with hips fully extended, no sagging, heels on a raised surface (30cm bench). Feels solid.
Single-Leg Hamstring Plank
 One heel on bench, other leg raised straight. Doubles the load per leg. Hips must stay level. 3 × 10s each side with hips level and no rotation. No dropping the raised leg for support.
Nordic Hamstring Curl Negative
 Heels anchored, lower body toward floor as slowly as possible, arms catch at the bottom. Brutal eccentric. 3 × 4 reps with 5s+ descent. One of the most effective hamstring strength builders available.
Full Nordic Hamstring Curl
 Full rep — lower and raise under hamstring power only. Exceptional posterior chain strength. Feeds pistol squat directly. 3 × 5 clean reps. Then add load or tempo.
