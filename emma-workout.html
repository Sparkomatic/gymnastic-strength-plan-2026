Key
Progressive overload is the whole game. Each week aim to do slightly more than last week — one more rep, a slightly heavier KB, or a harder variation. Without this the body adapts and stops changing. Log your weights and reps so you can track progress.
DAY 1
Lower Body & Glutes
Strength
Main Session
35–40 min
Exercise Sets × Reps Tempo Rest Notes
KB Goblet Squat ▶ Video
Primary quad + glute builder. Foundation of the lower body work.
 3 × 12 3s down · 1s pause · 2s up 90s Hold KB at chest. Chest tall throughout. Knees track over toes. Heels flat. Go heavier as this gets easy.
KB Romanian Deadlift ▶ Video
Hamstring and glute mass. The best posterior chain exercise for this goal.
 3 × 10–12 3s lower · 1s return 90s Hinge at hips — don't squat down. Slight bend in knees. Feel the hamstring stretch at the bottom. Keep spine neutral.
Hip Thrust ▶ Video
Direct glute loading. The most effective exercise for glute development.
 3 × 15 2s up · 2s hold at top · 2s down 60s Back on bench or sofa. KB across hips. Squeeze glutes at top — don't just extend the back. Drive through heels.
SSReverse Lunge ▶ Video
Single-leg strength. Reveals and fixes imbalances between legs.
 3 × 10 each Controlled descent · 2s return — Step back, not forward — easier on the knee. Back knee just above floor. Front heel stays flat. Add KB when bodyweight is easy.
SSSingle-Leg Calf Raise ▶ Video
Calf strength and shape. Often neglected but very visible.
 3 × 12–15 each 1s up · 3s lower 60s after pair Off a step edge for full range. Slow eccentric (lowering) phase is where the work is. Hold wall for balance only.
Superset SS: do Reverse Lunge immediately into Single-Leg Calf Raise with no rest between, then rest 60s before the next round.
Evening — Flexibility
10–15 min
StretchHoldNotes
Hip Flexor Stretch ▶ Video
 2 × 45s each Kneeling lunge. Tuck the pelvis under slightly to feel it in the front of the hip, not the thigh.
Hamstring Floor Stretch ▶ Video
 2 × 45s each Lying on back, pull one leg toward you with a strap or towel. Knee slightly soft. Feel the stretch in the back of the thigh.
Pigeon Pose or Figure-4 ▶ Video
 2 × 60s each Glute and hip external rotator stretch. Figure-4 on back is easier on the knee if pigeon is uncomfortable.
DAY 2
Upper Body & Core
Strength
Main Session
35–40 min
Exercise Sets × Reps Tempo Rest Notes
SS ARing Row (or Table Row) ▶ Video
Back, rear delts, biceps. The pulling movement women most often skip.
 3 × 10–12 2s pull · 1s hold · 3s lower — Body at 45° to floor. Pull rings to lower chest. Squeeze shoulder blades at top. Harder = more horizontal body. Easier = more upright.
SS APush-Up ▶ Video
Chest, shoulders, triceps. Progress from knees → feet → feet elevated.
 3 × 8–12 3s down · 1s up 90s after pair Start on knees if needed. Hands slightly wider than shoulders. Elbows at ~45°. When 12 knee reps feels easy, move to feet.
KB Single-Arm Shoulder Press ▶ Video
Shoulder strength and shape. Single-arm forces core stability too.
 3 × 10 each 2s up · 2s down 60s between arms Stand or sit. Brace core before pressing. Don't lean to the side. Press directly overhead, not in front.
SS BDead Bug ▶ Video
Deep core stability. Trains the abs to resist movement — more functional than crunches.
 3 × 6 each side Slow and controlled — Lower back MUST stay flat on floor — if it arches, the range is too big. Breathe out as you extend. Opposite arm and leg together.
SS BSide Plank ▶ Video
Lateral core and glute medius strength. Oblique definition and hip stability.
 3 × 25–30s each Hold 60s after pair From knees (easier) or feet (harder). Hips forward, body in a straight line. Don't let hips sag or pike up.
Band Pull-Apart ▶ Video
Rear delt and upper back. Directly counteracts the forward posture from sitting.
 3 × 15–20 Slow controlled pull · 2s return 45s Arms straight out in front, light band. Pull apart to a T position. Squeeze shoulder blades. Don't shrug. If no band: substitute with a towel row.
DAY 3
Full Body + Metabolic
Strength
Main Session
40–45 min
Exercise Sets × Reps Tempo Rest Notes
KB Deadlift ▶ Video
Total body compound. Hip hinge pattern. Builds everything — glutes, hamstrings, back, grip.
 3 × 10 3s lower · 1s return 90s KB between feet. Push the floor away rather than pulling the weight up. Hips and shoulders rise together. Don't round the lower back.
KB Swing ▶ Video
Hip power and cardiovascular conditioning. Burns calories and builds glutes simultaneously.
 3 × 15 Explosive hip drive 60s This is a hip hinge, not a squat. Power comes from the hips snapping forward, not the arms pulling. KB should float, not be muscled up.
SSGoblet Squat ▶ Video
Compound leg work to maintain lower body stimulus alongside the upper body focus.
 3 × 12 3s down · 2s up — Same as Day 1. Try to use a heavier KB than Day 1 if possible — you're more warmed up.
SSRing or Floor Push-Up ▶ Video
Horizontal push — maintains upper body work without needing a second upper body day.
 3 × 10 3s down · 1s up 90s after pair Progress to ring push-ups when floor push-ups feel easy — the instability dramatically increases the stimulus.
Hollow Body Hold ▶ Video
Gymnastic core — trains the abs as stabilisers under load, not just as crunchers.
 3 × 20–30s Hold. Don't let the lower back arch. 45s Arms overhead, legs straight and low. Lower back pressed into floor. If too hard, bend knees or raise legs higher. Build duration over weeks.
This session ties everything together — lower body, upper body, core, and conditioning in one. It's the most time-efficient session of the week.
Finisher — KB Circuit
10 min
FormatRoundsExercises
40s on · 20s rest 3 rounds KB Swings → Goblet Squat → Push-Up → Mountain Climbers → Rest 90s between rounds. Keep intensity high — this is the conditioning stimulus.
DAY 4
Active Conditioning
Optional
Choose One
20–40 min
OptionDuration / FormatWhy
Brisk Walk or Hike
 30–45 min Best low-impact calorie burn that doesn't interfere with muscle recovery. Particularly effective for fat loss without adding fatigue to the training days.
Cycling (easy pace)
 20–30 min Low impact, keeps legs moving without heavy loading. Easy enough to recover from before Day 1 of the next week.
Yoga / Mobility Flow
 20–30 min Active recovery. Improves flexibility and reduces soreness. Any general yoga class or YouTube video works.
Light Full-Body Circuit
 3 rounds Bodyweight squats × 15, push-ups × 10, mountain climbers × 20, plank × 30s. Low weight, high rep. Not a hard session — just movement.
Day 4 is genuinely optional. If energy is low or life is busy, skip it — the three main sessions do all the meaningful work. Never sacrifice sleep or recovery for this session.

What to expect and when
→ Weeks 1–2: Learning the movements. Some soreness. Energy may dip.
→ Weeks 3–4: Movements feel natural. Strength increases noticeably.
→ Weeks 5–8: Visible body composition changes begin. Keep adding load.
→ Months 3–6: Significant strength and physique transformation if nutrition is dialled in.
